## Segment 4: GLM 5.2, Open Weights, and Chinese Models

Dan:

The third piece is GLM 5.2.

This is where the conversation gets bigger than one benchmark.

Because GLM 5.2 is part of the Chinese open-weight model wave.

So let me explain the difference.

Open source means you get the code, the ability to inspect it, modify it, and usually redistribute it under a license.

Open weight means you get the trained model weights. You may be able to run it yourself, fine-tune it, or host it in your own environment. But you usually do not get the full training data or the full recipe.

In AI, a lot of people casually say "open source" when what they really mean is open weight.

That distinction matters.

Because open-weight models are the bridge between closed frontier models and fully private AI infrastructure.

What your users ask.

What workflows you are automating.

What your product is good at.

Where your customers struggle.

And if the model becomes capable of doing the thing your software does, you have to at least ask a brutal question:

Am I paying my future competitor to learn my business?

That is why some US companies are now seriously looking at Chinese open-weight models, local hosting, and non-frontier model routing.

Not because China is automatically safer.

Because closed-model dependence is also not safe.

## Segment 5: Sovereign AI and the Compute Cost Hedge

Dan:

This is also where sovereign AI becomes more than a government buzzword.

Sovereign AI means countries, companies, and industries want AI systems they can control.

Not every query going through the same three American model labs.

Not every business model dependent on the same API pricing page.

Not every workflow exposed to the same closed vendor.

For Canada, this matters.

For real estate, it matters too.

Because if we are building a real estate investor AI platform, the valuable data is not the public listing description.

The valuable data is:

- what the investor believed at the start
- what assumptions they changed
- what an architect corrected
- what the lender said
- what the property actually sold for
- which lead converted
- which expert was useful
- which underwriting assumption was wrong

That data should not casually leak into every model vendor's training and inference loop.

So the future is probably not one model.

It is a routing system.

Hosted frontier models when you need premium reasoning.

Reliable closed models when you need workflow completion.

Cheap agentic models when you need scale.

Open-weight models when you need cost control, private deployment, or leverage against rising inference costs.

That is the sovereign-AI angle.

Not flag-waving.

Control.
