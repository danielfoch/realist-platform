# Local Expert Invitation Email

Personalized invite for existing meetup hosts / local pros to claim their market as the
exclusive Realist Local Expert. Recruiting page: **realist.ca/local-experts** (flow lands at
`/partner/onboarding`). Roster + markets: [`meetup-hosts.md`](../meetup-hosts.md).

---

**Subject:** Want to claim [City] before someone else does?

**Preview text:** Your market. First dibs is yours.

**Body:**

> Hey [First Name],
>
> Quick one, because I know you're busy.
>
> Every time I think about [City], the same name comes up: yours. You're the person people in
> that room actually trust. So before I open this up to anyone else, I wanted to bring it to you
> first.
>
> Here's the thing we're doing on Realist: **one local expert per market.** Not a directory of
> twenty names — one. The person we point every investor to when they're buying in your city. For
> [City], I'd rather that be a host who's already in the room every month than a stranger who
> filled out a form.
>
> The mechanic is simple. Investors from the podcast, YouTube, and the newsletter underwrite real
> deals on Realist — address, strategy, rents, cash flow, the whole thing. When one of them is
> buying in your market, that lead routes to you **with the full underwriting attached.** You're
> not chasing a name and a phone number. You're getting a deal that's already been run.
>
> What it costs to join: nothing. No monthly fee. You only pay when a referred deal actually
> closes. [FEE_LINE] Cancel anytime.
>
> Two things so it's clean:
>
> - Anything within about two hours of Toronto we keep in-house at Valery. Everywhere else — your
>   market — routes to the local expert. That's you.
> - One ask in return: keep your deal statuses current in the CRM we give you. If a lead goes
>   quiet for a couple weeks, routing just pauses until you catch up — not a penalty, just so

>   pipeline through anyway.
>
> You already host the room, and we keep promoting it across the network. This just makes it pay.
>
> Grab [City] here — takes about two minutes: **realist.ca/local-experts**
>
> Once it's claimed, it's gone. But no pressure either way — if you've got questions, just hit
> reply, it comes straight to me. And I'll see you at the next one regardless.
>
> Dan

## How to send it

**Merge fields:** swap `[First Name]` and every `[City]` with the recipient's name and market.

**Resolve `[FEE_LINE]` by partner type — never send it unresolved:**

- **Realtors:** "For realtors that's 25% of the gross commission on closed deals, payable to
  Valery Real Estate Inc., on a standard OREA 641-style referral agreement you sign online in a
  couple minutes."
- **Mortgage pros** (emails at `stanmtg.ca`, `cleartrust.ca`, or anyone on the mortgage side):
  "For the mortgage side that's 50% on funded deals, payable to BLD Financial — Nick Hill's shop,
  so you're in good hands. Signed online in a couple minutes." **Do not** show mortgage
  recipients the OREA/realtor language.

**Routing guard:** only send to hosts whose market is **outside** the ~2-hour-from-Toronto
in-house zone. Anyone inside routes to Valery in-house and shouldn't get this.

**Meetup fee is separate:** this invite is about the **free** Realist Local Expert referral
access only. Hosts keep paying their $250/mo meetup fee as usual — the email doesn't mention or
change it. The "no monthly fee" line refers strictly to the referral access. If a recipient asks,
be clear: meetup hosting is unchanged; the expert/referral access is a free add-on.

**Warmth upgrade (high-leverage):** the first paragraph is generic on purpose. If you have a real
shared moment — a talk they gave, a deal they mentioned, the last meetup you both spoke at —
replace "Every time I think about [City]" with that specific memory. If they co-host, nod to it
("you and [co-host] run a great room").

**Sign-off:** keep "Dan" for people you're genuinely close with; use "Daniel Foch" for the more
formal ones.
