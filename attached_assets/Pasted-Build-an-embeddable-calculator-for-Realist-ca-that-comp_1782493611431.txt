Build an embeddable calculator for Realist.ca that compares buying a home with a normal CMHC-insured mortgage versus a rent-to-own model like KeyOwn.

Goal:
Create a clean, mobile-friendly interactive calculator for a Canadian real estate article/video page. The calculator should help users compare the upfront cash, monthly cost, equity path, and total 5-year cost of:

1. Traditional purchase with insured mortgage
2. Rent-to-own pathway

First inspect the existing Realist.ca codebase and match the current stack, styling, routing, and component patterns. Do not redesign the site. Build the simplest working component that can be dropped into an article or landing page.

Inputs:
- Home price, default $600,000
- Mortgage interest rate, default 5.00%
- Amortization, default 25 years
- Traditional buyer down payment %, default 5%
- CMHC insurance premium %, default 4% when down payment is 5%; make editable
- Property tax annual %, default 1.0%
- Condo fee monthly, default $600
- Insurance/utilities monthly, default $250
- Rent-to-own initial contribution %, default 2.5%
- Rent-to-own target final down payment %, default 10%
- Rent-to-own term, default 5 years
- Rent-to-own monthly occupancy payment, default $2,800
- Rent-to-own monthly equity contribution, default auto-calculated as the gap between initial contribution and target final down payment divided by months
- Expected annual home appreciation %, default 0%

Traditional mortgage calculations:
- Down payment dollars = price * traditional down payment %
- Base mortgage = price - down payment
- CMHC premium = base mortgage * CMHC premium %
- Total mortgage = base mortgage + CMHC premium
- Monthly mortgage payment using standard amortizing loan formula
- Monthly carrying cost = mortgage payment + condo fee + property tax monthly + insurance/utilities
- 5-year mortgage payments total
- Estimated remaining mortgage balance after 5 years
- Estimated property value after 5 years using appreciation assumption
- Estimated owner equity after 5 years = property value - remaining mortgage balance

Rent-to-own calculations:
- Initial contribution dollars = price * initial contribution %
- Target final down payment dollars = future property value or current price * target final down payment %. Use a toggle or note; simplest version can use current price.
- Equity gap = target final down payment - initial contribution
- Monthly equity contribution = equity gap / term months unless user overrides
- Monthly rent-to-own payment = occupancy payment + equity contribution
- 5-year occupancy payments total
- 5-year equity contributions total
- Total cash paid over term = initial contribution + occupancy payments + equity contributions
- Estimated accumulated equity at end = initial contribution + equity contributions

Output cards:
- Upfront cash required: traditional vs rent-to-own
- Monthly payment: traditional carrying cost vs rent-to-own monthly payment
- 5-year total cash outlay
- Estimated equity after 5 years
- Main tradeoff summary in plain English

Design requirements:
- Realist.ca style: clean, editorial, data-driven, no cartoonish styling
- Responsive layout
- Use sliders for percentages and rates where appropriate
- Use currency formatting in CAD
- Use clear labels and small explanatory helper text
- Include a small disclaimer: “Illustrative only. Not financial, mortgage, tax, or legal advice. Rent-to-own program terms vary materially.”
- Include a “Reset assumptions” button
- Include a “Copy scenario summary” button that copies a text summary to clipboard
