## Page types to build

### 1. Market pages (programmatic)

Create pages for:

* Canada
* Province

Each page includes:

* current pricing trends
* inventory levels

### 2. Query-driven pages

Target high-intent SEO queries like:

* “are condo prices dropping in Toronto”
* “new construction price drops Canada”
* “pre construction condos Toronto price cuts”
* “is it a good time to buy pre construction Canada”
* “Toronto condo inventory levels 2026”
* “are builders lowering prices Ontario”

Generate pages like:

* /toronto-condo-prices-dropping
* /canada-preconstruction-price-cuts
* /ontario-new-build-prices

Each page:

* answers the query directly in first 2 paragraphs
* includes supporting data and charts
* includes citations to internal datasets (not external fluff)

### 3. Project-level pages (high leverage)

Create pages for:

* individual developments
* builder portfolios

Example:

* /projects/q-tower-toronto
* /projects/creekside-condos-milton

Each page includes:

* current pricing
* price changes over time
* comparison to resale
* incentives (if detectable)

These pages can rank extremely well long-term.

### 4. “Best deals” / ranking pages

## Content generation rules

Every page should include:

### 1. SEO header structure

* H1: exact keyword match
* H2s: variations and related queries
* H3s: supporting data sections

### 2. Opening paragraph (VERY IMPORTANT)

* answer the search query immediately
* include keyword naturally
* do not fluff

Example:
“Toronto condo prices are declining in 2026, with new construction sales at a 35-year low and inventory at record highs. Recent data shows price cuts outnumber increases across active developments.”

---

### 3. Data sections

Each page must include:

* charts (price trends, inventory, price changes)
* tables (top movers, rankings)
* key stats (cut:raise ratio, avg change, etc.)

---

### 4. Internal linking

Automatically link to:

* related cities
* related reports
* project pages
* tools (deal analyzer, cndo.com, etc.)

---

### 5. CTA placement

Include:

* early soft CTA (tools, data)
* mid-page CTA (analysis tools)
* end CTA (signup, platform, etc.)
